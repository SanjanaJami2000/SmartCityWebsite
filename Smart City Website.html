Visakhapatnam is a port city and industrial center in the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh, on the Bay of Bengal. It's known for its many beaches, including Ramakrishna Beach, home to a preserved submarine at the Kursura Submarine Museum. Nearby are the elaborate Kali Temple and the Visakha Museum, an old Dutch bungalow housing local maritime and historical exhibits.     2.Araku



    Araku is a famous hill station in Andhra Pradesh, people from different places plan to visit this lush green valley with waterfalls all around.
    It is very cool and temperatures go sub-zero during winters.
    People visit during winter months( November end-Jan beginning)
    There are lot of spots to visit in Araku:

    • 1)Chapprai waterfalls



      • It is called "Chapprai" bcoz, instead of waterfall falling from the heights, it will slide down on the ground like a mat.

      Entry fees="None"
      Timings="9Am to 4:30Pm"
      Best time to visit="December"

    • 2)katiki waterfalls



      • Beautiful waterfalls with trees and bushes all around.
      The most exciting is : trekking
      You need to trek for a long time to reach to this waterfall

      Entry fees="none"
      Timings="10Am to 4:30Pm"
      Best time to visit="December"

    • 3)kothapalli waterfalls


      • Entry fees="none"
      Timings="10:30Am to 4:30Pm"
      Best time to visit="Decemebr"

    • 4)coffee museum



      • Araku coffee is a pure arabica with a rare aromatic profile combining smoothness, balance and roundness.
      It is created by our French coffeeologist Hippolyte Courty, guaranteeing utmost quality at each step, from the plantation to the cup.

      Entry fees="Adults:40rs, kids:"20rs"
      timings="10Am to 5Pm",
      Best time to visit="December"

    • 5)tribal museum



      • Entry fees="Adults:40rs,Kids:20rs"

    • 6)padmapuram gardens



      • Entry fees="10rs"
      Timings="9Am to 5Pm"
      Best time to visit="December"

    • 7)borra caves



      • One of the largest caves in the country, Borra Caves are home to million-year-old stalactite and stalagmite formations.
      They came into existence as a result of the flow of Gosthani river on the limestone deposits in the area.
      These caves were discovered by William King George of the Geological Survey of India.
      Legend has it that Borra Caves was first discovered when a cowherd, while searching for one of his lost cows, accidentally came across them.
      There he found a shivling deep inside the cave, along with his cow. He believed that Lord Shiva had saved his cow.
      Later, villagers built a small temple just outside the caves.

      Entry fees="free"
      Timings: 10Am to 3:30Pm"
      Best time to visit:"December"

    7.Ramanaidu Studios




    The Rama Naidu studios, which are located near the beautiful Bay of Bengal, have been used to film a number of South Indian films.The view of the surrounding area will be an added bonus to your tour. Enjoy your time here taking photos and videos. you will undoubtedly bring back wonderful memories.

    The Ramanaidu film studio is located on the beach road connecting Visakhapatnam and Bheemili, 18 kilometres from RK beach. This film studio, which is surrounded on one side by hills and on the other by the sea, is used by several film producers from neighbouring states to produce films in national and regional languages.

    Entry fees="35 Rupees"
    Timings="9:30Am to 5Pm"

    8.Zoo Park




    Also called as "Indira Gandhi Zoological Park".Surrounded by the Eastern Ghats on three sides and set in the laps of the Kambalakonda Forest Reserve this park is spread over a luxurious 625 acres and
    is home to almost 80 species of wildlife, making it one of the largest zoological parks in Andhra Pradesh.

    With the Bay of Bengal nearby, the location of this park makes it an ideal spot for spotting several exotic bird species too. The weather and the climatic conditions of this area have allowed the fauna in the zoo to flourish and create a salubrious home for the animals.

    Entry fees="70 Rupees(Adults)
    30 Rupees(kids)"

    Timings="10Am to 5Pm"
